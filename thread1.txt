## **PART A: WEB ACCESSIBILITY OBLIGATIONS FROM TENDER**

### **Key Finding: Mandatory Compliance Requirements**

The tender contains **explicit and binding obligations** for web accessibility, found in **Bilag F (Appendix F) - Ydelsesområder**, specifically within the UX-design service areas for both traditional and agile development approaches.

### **1. Primary Obligation: Legal Compliance**

**Requirement (Danish):**
> "Sikring af overholdelse af Loven om tilgængelighed af offentlige organers websteder og mobilapplikationer (Web-tilgængelighed)."

**Translation:**
> "Ensuring compliance with the Law on Accessibility of Public Sector Websites and Mobile Applications (Web Accessibility)."

**Location:**
- Document: Bilag F Ydelsesområder.pdf
- Section 3.5.1 (Page 31): Ydelsesområde 12 - Traditional waterfall development
- Section 3.5.2 (Page 33): Ydelsesområde 12 - Agile development

**What This Means:**
All IT consulting services involving UX design must ensure developed solutions comply with Danish web accessibility law. This applies to:
- New system development (nyt fagsystem)
- Mobile application development
- Configuration of new standard systems
- Adjustments to newly developed systems
- Both waterfall and agile methodologies

### **2. WAS-Tool Implementation Requirement**

**Requirement (Danish):**
> "Udfærdigelse af lovpligtige erklæringer om web-tilgængelighed under anvendelse af Digitaliseringsstyrelsens WAS-tool."

**Translation:**
> "Preparation of legally required statements on web accessibility using the Danish Agency for Digital Government's WAS-tool."

**Location:** Same sections as above (Pages 31 and 33 of Bilag F)

**What This Means:**
Suppliers MUST use the official government WAS-tool (Web Accessibility Statement Tool) to:
- Create standardized accessibility statements (tilgængelighedserklæringer)
- Document conformance levels
- Report on non-accessible content with justifications
- Provide user feedback mechanisms

**WAS-Tool Access:** https://www.was.digst.dk/

### **3. Scope & Application**

✅ **Applies to:**
- All new development projects requiring UX design
- Both websites and mobile applications
- Traditional and agile development approaches
- Projects for all customer segments (Kommune, Region, Stat, Øvrige)

❌ **Not explicitly mentioned:**
- Specific WCAG level requirements (though law mandates WCAG 2.1 AA)
- Compliance deadlines (beyond general project timelines)
- Testing procedures or audit requirements
- Remediation requirements for existing systems

## **PART B: CURRENT DANISH LEGAL FRAMEWORK & OFFICIAL GUIDANCE**

### **1. Primary Legislation**

**Lov om tilgængelighed af offentlige organers websteder og mobilapplikationer**
- **Citation:** LBK nr 692 af 23/05/2018
- **Official Source:** https://www.retsinformation.dk/eli/lta/2018/692
- **Status:** In force (Danish implementation of EU Directive 2016/2102)
- **Common Name:** Webtilgængelighedsloven

**Key Points:**
- Mandates accessibility for all public sector websites and mobile apps
- Requires publication of accessibility statements
- Establishes monitoring and enforcement mechanisms
- Applies to all entities in Bilag A customer list

### **2. EU Legal Basis**

**Directive (EU) 2016/2102** - Web Accessibility Directive
- **Adopted:** October 26, 2016
- **EUR-Lex:** https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/en/NIM/?uri=oj:JOL_2016_327_R_0001
- **Implementation Deadline:** September 23, 2018
